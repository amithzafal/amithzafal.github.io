Welcome to my page

Greetings! I'm Amith, a researcher at Brandeis University, Boston. My current research focuses on understanding the dynamics of epigenetic states at the single-cell level, a pursuit that combines molecular biology experiments with computational modeling. Developing computational models to have a better mechanistic know-how of the fundamental processes driving chromatin folding and gene regulation is of great interest to me. My research interests span a wide spectrum, from cellular processes to collective behavior in active matter, in a broad sense deciphering the physics of biological systems fascinate me.

If you'd like to read about epigenetic patterns letting DNA artfully change its expression, delve into "Painters in chromatin". Or if you prefer, a short talk.

Some of my works:

  • 4D epigenomics: deciphering the coupling between genome folding and epigenomic regulation with biophysical modeling, Curr Opin Genet Dev 2023
  • Painters in chromatin: a unified quantitative framework to characterize epigenome regulation and memory, Nulceic Acids Research 2022 (arXiv)
  • Dynamical modeling of the H3K27 epigenetic landscape in mouse embryonic stem cells, PLoS CB 2022 (arXiv)
  • Contact Force Mediated Rapid Deposition of Colloidal Microspheres Flowing over Microstructured Barriers, Langmuir 2021
  • Swimming statistics of cargo-loaded single bacteria, Soft Matter 2020
  • Active modulation of surfactant-driven flow instabilities by swarming bacteria, Physical Review E 2020
  • Tuning the torque-speed characteristics of the bacterial flagellar motor to enhance swimming speed, Physical Review E 2019
